TALKING TO YOUR PATIENTS ABOUT FACE LIFTS!

When your patients enter your office feeling old and looking stressed, there are a few different cosmetic procedures you can offer them. The most transformative might be a face lift surgery; however, you may also suggest less-invasive procedures. You need to talk to your patients first to understand how you can best help them.

Discuss the Reasons for the Procedure

Many of your patients will come to you to reduce the signs of aging across their body. Most of these signs occur on the face, usually due to sagging skin and wrinkles around the eyes and mouth. One of the best and more permanent cosmetic procedures to reverse the signs of aging is through a face lift surgery. Discuss the reasons your patients wish to have a face lift versus other cosmetic procedures. This discussion will help you and your patients to understand the procedure and the realistic expectations.

Discuss the Results

Face lift surgery can give your patients a younger, fresher appearance, resulting an increased self-esteem and confidence. Your patients may experience a reversal of aging signs, stress lines, and signs of sun exposure. Results can last many years, keeping your patients happy and looking younger for a long time. Based on each of your patients’ individual histories, discuss the realistic results they can expect to see.

Discuss the Alternatives

Depending on your patient discussions, consider advising on the alternatives to a face lift surgery. For example, if a patient’s chief complaint is about wrinkles around the mouth, then you might suggest a series of injectable solutions that can temporarily reverse the signs of wrinkles. If your patient dislikes an area of the face, then you might suggest a surgery specific to correcting that area, rather than an entire face lift surgery.
